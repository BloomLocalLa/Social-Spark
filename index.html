Educational: Mulch Guide
The Complete Mulch Guide: Types, Benefits, and How Much You Need Mulch isn't just decorative - it's one of the hardest working elements in your landscape. BENEFITS: - Retains moisture (less watering) - Suppresses weeds (less pulling) - Regulates soil temperature - Adds nutrients as it breaks down - Makes everything look polished TYPES WE USE: Hardwood Mulch: Our go-to. Rich color, breaks down slowly, great for beds. Pine Bark: Acidic, great for azaleas and camellias. Pine Straw: Classic Southern look, excellent for slopes. HOW MUCH? 2-3 inches is the sweet spot. Less than that and weeds break through. More than that and you're suffocating roots. HOW TO CALCULATE: Measure your bed (length x width = square feet) Divide by 100 Multiply by 3 (for 3" depth) = cubic yards needed Or just call us and we'll handle everything. Questions about mulch? Drop them below!

Educational: Mulch Deep Dive
Mulch Mistakes That Are Hurting Your Plants Mulch is amazing for your landscape - when done right. Here are the mistakes we see all the time: MISTAKE #1: TOO DEEP More than 3-4 inches suffocates roots and causes rot. We see beds with 6+ inches of mulch all the time. More is not better. MISTAKE #2: VOLCANO MULCHING Piling mulch against tree trunks holds moisture against bark and invites disease. Pull it back 3-4 inches from the trunk. MISTAKE #3: WRONG TYPE Dyed mulch fades fast and can leach chemicals. Fresh hardwood or pine bark mulch breaks down naturally and feeds your soil. MISTAKE #4: NEVER REFRESHING Old mulch compacts and stops doing its job. Refresh annually for best results. MISTAKE #5: SKIPPING EDGING Mulch without clean edges looks sloppy. Edge first, then mulch. The good news? All of these are easy fixes. Which of these have you been guilty of? No judgment - we've all been there!

Getting Your Landscape Ready for Summer Heat Louisiana summer is coming. Here's how to prepare your yard: PLANT SMART (OR WAIT) If you're planting now, stick to heat-lovers: Lantana, Pentas, Vinca, Ornamental Grasses. Or wait until fall for major installs. MULCH IS YOUR FRIEND 3 inches of mulch keeps roots cool and retains moisture. If yours is thin or faded, now is the time to refresh. WATER DEEPLY, LESS OFTEN Shallow daily watering creates weak roots. Deep watering 2-3x per week encourages roots to grow down where it's cooler. SET UP YOUR IRRIGATION If you have an irrigation system, check it NOW. Fix broken heads, adjust coverage, set timers. PRUNE NOW, NOT LATER Shape up anything that needs it before the real heat hits. Pruning in summer stress can damage plants. PLAN FOR SHADE Know where the brutal afternoon sun hits? Consider shade solutions for you AND your plants. What's on your summer prep list?

Educational: Summer Maintenance
Summer Maintenance Mode: What Your Yard Needs Now Spring planting season is wrapping up. We're shifting into summer maintenance mode. Here's what that means: WATERING Most important thing this time of year. Deep watering 2-3x per week beats shallow daily watering. Early morning is best - less evaporation. MULCH CHECK If your mulch is thin, now is the time to top it off. It's insulation for your plant roots. WEED PATROL Stay on top of weeds before they take over. A few minutes of pulling now saves hours later. DEADHEADING Remove spent flowers to encourage more blooms. Works great for Knock Out Roses, Pentas, and most flowering plants. HOLD OFF ON PRUNING Major pruning in summer heat stresses plants. Just remove dead or damaged material. NO NEW PLANTING Wait until fall for major installs. Summer is too stressful for new plants. PEST MONITORING Keep an eye out for issues. Catching problems early is key. Summer maintenance isn't complicated - it's consistent. A little attention each week keeps things healthy until fall. Creating a Kid-Friendly Backyard School's out (or almost out). Kids are going to be home. A lot. Here's how to make your backyard actually work for family life: CLEAR PLAY SPACE An open area for running, playing, and general chaos. Doesn't have to be huge, just intentional. Or alternative groundcovers in high-traffic areas. Mulch paths work great. VISIBILITY Can you see the play area from inside or from your relaxation spot? Line of sight matters.
